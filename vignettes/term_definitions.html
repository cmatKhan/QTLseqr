Definition of Terms

Introduction

This vignette serves to define terms used in both the code and documentation of this package, and includes some discussion on terms that are commonly used in the Bulk Segregant Analysis (BSA) and Quantitative Trait Locus (QTL) literature which I chose to re-name in this package.

QTL-seq Terms

The terms below are derived from Takagi et al. (2013)

Traditional Term Term Used in This Package Description/Notes
Bulk (Low, High) population_1, population_2 population refers to a group of samples pooled together for sequencing. Enter ?createComparisonFrame in your console for more details.
SNP-Index alt_frequency The ratio of the number of reads containing the alternate allele to the total number of reads at a SNP.
\(\Delta\)(SNP-Index) delta_alt_frequency The difference in alt allele frequency between population_2 and population_1, i.e. population_2_alt_frequency - population_1_alt_frequency

BSA Statistics Terms

The terms below are derived from Magwene (2011)

Traditional Term Term Used in This Package Description/Notes
G G no change here – I have no problem with this name
G’ G smooth This will be some variation of GSmooth or g_smooth in the code. It is the result of local regression smoothing on the G metric
